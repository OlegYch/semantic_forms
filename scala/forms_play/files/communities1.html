We like to have a concept-centered view, which shows what happened recently on that. Thanks to the new /history?uri= feature, that's exactly what we can do. Precisely it shows the URI's reached from there by RDF paths of length <= 2, sorted in the historical order. This can be seen as a way of expliciting communities of interest. We can focus on any URI: an entry from Wikipedia / dbPedia, a project, a person, etc.

Examples:

  • During the biodiversity day at ISWC a biodiversity community was launched: Biodiversity community
  • NLP (Natural Language Processing) NLP interest group
  • everything related to software development: developers community
